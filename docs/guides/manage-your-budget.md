# Manage your budget

**This guide shows how to track spending and plan a budget in AIPA using the Finance tool, then set aside money with the Savings Tracker.** These tools are part of the [Finance & Health](/features/finance-and-health) category, and you enter the data yourself — AIPA does not connect to your bank.

## Steps

1. **Open Finance.** From the Features launcher, search "finance" or "budget" and open it.
2. **Log income and expenses.** Add transactions as they happen, with categories so totals make sense.
3. **Set category budgets.** Decide a monthly limit per category (food, transport, subscriptions) and let the tool show you how you're tracking.
4. **Review weekly.** A short weekly check-in catches overspending early, before the month is gone.
5. **Move money to savings.** Create a goal in the **Savings Tracker** and update its progress when you set money aside.

## Tips

- **Log promptly.** The closer to the moment you record a purchase, the more accurate your picture.
- **Start with a few categories.** Too many categories make tracking a chore; refine over time.
- **Name your savings goal.** A goal called "Emergency fund" or "New laptop" is easier to fund than a vague number.
- **Pair with [Goals](/features/productivity).** Tie a savings target to a broader personal goal.

## Honest note

AIPA is **not financial advice** and does not connect to your bank. It helps you record and plan your own money privately. For investment, tax, or financial-planning decisions, talk to a qualified professional.

## FAQ

### Does it import transactions from my bank?
No. You enter transactions yourself, which keeps your financial data private and the tool free.

### Can I track several savings goals?
Yes — create multiple goals in the Savings Tracker and update each independently.

### Does my budget sync across devices?
Yes, when signed in. See [Account & Sync](/getting-started/account-and-sync).
